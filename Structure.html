Business

Our system uses sensors, AI, and IoT to improve traffic flow and reduce wasted fuel. It offers adaptive traffic timing and alternative routes. The model reduces waiting time by 71% and improves flow efficiency by 36%.

Scientific Methodology

Our system uses a combination of AI, IoT, and sensor networks to manage traffic efficiently. The central hub, an ESP32 microcontroller, processes real-time data from ultrasonic sensors, ESP32 CAM modules, MQ135 gas sensors, and LDR sensors.

The ESP32 CAM captures live traffic images and uses a lightweight YOLO model to detect vehicle types and congestion levels. Ultrasonic sensors measure vehicle distance and density, while MQ135 sensors monitor COâ‚‚ levels, ensuring environmental monitoring. Emergency lanes remain closed until congestion rises or emergency alerts are triggered.

Day/night conditions are handled with LDR sensors, and LCD displays dynamically show traffic directions. Servo motors operate barriers to divert traffic when needed. Automated test vehicles simulate real traffic, using ultrasonic sensors and servo-driven obstacle avoidance.

The system has proven to reduce waiting time by 71.43%, lower traffic density and air pollution (from 60 ppm to 12 ppm), and improve trip flow efficiency by 36%.

Key Components:

  • ESP32 Central Hub
  • ESP32 CAM for real-time monitoring
  • MQ135 Gas Sensor for pollution detection
  • Ultrasonic Sensors for vehicle detection
  • LDR Sensors for day/night detection
  • SG90 Servo Motors for traffic barriers
  • LCD Display for driver messages

Testing & Results:

  • Prototype tested across low, medium, and high traffic phases
  • Collected data on trip time, waiting time, and fuel consumption
  • AI analysis confirms reduced trip time and greenhouse gas emissions by ~65%

Overall, Traffinity provides predictive traffic management, immediate emergency response, and environmental monitoring, offering a safer and more efficient transportation system compared to traditional methods.
